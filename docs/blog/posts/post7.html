Why UUID v1 Matters in 2025

Why UUID v1 Is Still Relevant in 2025

Published: November 28, 2025

Many developers assume UUID v1 is obsolete due to MAC address privacy concerns. That was true in 2005 — but not anymore.

The Privacy Myth

RFC 4122 allows random nodes. When combined with randomization (like this tool does by default), UUID v1 has zero privacy risk — and massive benefits.

Why v1 Wins in Practice

  • Perfect chronological sorting without extra indexes
  • Instant timestamp extraction for debugging
  • Better database write performance than v4
  • Ideal for event sourcing, CQRS, analytics

Modern Stack Support

Major databases now recommend time-ordered IDs:

  • Cassandra, ScyllaDB → prefer time-based UUIDs
  • ClickHouse, Snowflake → leverage sort keys
  • PostgreSQL 17+ → native UUID v7 support (similar benefits)

Conclusion

UUID v1 isn’t dead — it’s evolved. With randomized nodes, it offers the best of both worlds: sortability + privacy + performance.

In 2025, UUID v1 isn’t legacy. It’s strategic.
